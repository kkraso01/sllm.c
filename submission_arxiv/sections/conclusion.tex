\section{Conclusion}
ALC provides a structured approach to inference-time adaptation through mutable in-model state with explicit persistence semantics. In the current evidence package, SessionKV-DR results support delayed recall and bounded update behavior relative to controls, and TinyQA provides secondary semi-realistic context.

The contribution is best understood as mechanism-level: a controllable alternative to weight-update-based adaptation in settings where stable runtime state is desirable. Broader external validity remains a follow-up objective rather than a claim established here.
