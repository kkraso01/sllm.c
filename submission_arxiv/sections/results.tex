\section{Results}
SessionKV-DR is the primary evidence source because benchmark success depends on write/retain/retrieve operations under delay and interference. We therefore interpret these results as mechanism-level validation rather than general NLP performance claims.

All reported comparisons include baseline, ALC no-write, and full ALC. This triad is essential: improvements over baseline support the memory hypothesis, while competitive no-write outcomes identify remaining write-policy and interference limits.

TinyQA results are reported as supporting semi-realistic evidence. They are useful for sanity checking language-shaped tasks, but they do not replace the targeted mechanism evaluations in SessionKV-DR.
