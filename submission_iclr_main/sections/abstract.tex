\begin{abstract}
We present the Adaptive Learning Component (ALC), a structured in-model memory mechanism for inference-time adaptation without modifying backbone weights. We evaluate ALC using SessionKV-DR, a benchmark designed to isolate delayed session-specific recall, overwrite behavior, persistence, and interference sensitivity. Across baseline, no-write, and full variants with multi-seed aggregation, results support mechanism-level improvements in delayed recall and stable update behavior in the tested setting. We explicitly scope these findings: current evidence is controlled and semi-realistic rather than broad benchmark coverage. The paper clarifies novelty boundaries relative to retrieval and weight-update adaptation, and outlines what larger-scale follow-up would need to establish.
\end{abstract}
