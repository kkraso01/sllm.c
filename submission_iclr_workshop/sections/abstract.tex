\begin{abstract}
We study the Adaptive Learning Component (ALC), a structured in-model memory mechanism for inference-time adaptation without weight updates. The paper centers on SessionKV-DR, a delayed-recall benchmark that directly probes writing session facts, retaining them under interference, retrieving them after delay, overwriting stale values, and preserving state across save/reload. Using baseline, ALC no-write, and full ALC comparisons with multi-seed aggregation, we find mechanism-level evidence that controlled writes improve delayed session-specific recall in this setup. TinyQA is retained as supporting semi-realistic evidence. We frame contributions conservatively: the results establish aligned evidence for the mechanism under controlled conditions and motivate broader follow-up evaluation.
\end{abstract}
