\section{Results}
We treat SessionKV-DR as the main evidence because it directly measures the intended mechanism. Delayed recall, overwrite, persistence, and capacity slices are all reported with baseline/no-write/full variants.

Performance gains for full ALC over baseline indicate effective inference-time state updates in this setup. Cases where no-write remains competitive are retained as important diagnostics, suggesting that routing/write policy robustness is not fully solved.

TinyQA outcomes are supportive and are interpreted as secondary to the mechanism-focused benchmark.
