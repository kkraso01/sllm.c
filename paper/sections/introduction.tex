\section{Introduction}
Many deployment settings require models to adapt to newly observed information during inference while preserving safety and reproducibility constraints that discourage direct weight updates. We investigate whether a compact, explicit memory mechanism can provide useful adaptation in this regime.

This paper reports a publication-oriented evaluation of the finalized Adaptive Learning Component (ALC) implementation in \texttt{llm.c}. ALC augments a transformer block with slot/key memory, differentiable routing (softmax or top-k softmax), and routing-weighted state updates. The mechanism is explicitly designed to support persistence and controlled behavior.

Our contribution in this pass is empirical and packaging-focused: a reproducible experiment suite, a paper-ready results pipeline, and an honest related-work positioning with conservative claims.
