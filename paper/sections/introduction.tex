\section{Introduction}
Many deployed settings require incorporating newly observed information during inference without post-deployment weight updates. Adaptive memory modules are one approach, but evaluation must match that claim directly.

This pass keeps the ALC mechanism fixed and focuses on claim-aligned evidence. Our main contribution here is benchmarking and packaging: we introduce SessionKV-DR, a benchmark that explicitly tests write, retention under delay/interference, retrieval, overwrite/update behavior, and persistence across save/reload. We report baseline, ALC no-write, and full ALC side-by-side with multi-seed aggregation.

We retain TinyQA and prior diagnostics, but reposition them as supporting evidence. The primary question is not generic QA quality; it is whether structured inference-time memory writes causally improve delayed session-specific recall and update behavior.
